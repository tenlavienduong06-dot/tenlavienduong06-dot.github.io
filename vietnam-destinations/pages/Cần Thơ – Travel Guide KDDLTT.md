**Cần Thơ – A Journey Through River Life** 

**1\. Overview**

At first sight, Can Tho appears lively and dynamic, where daily trade unfolds along the vast Hau River. However, beyond its energetic urban rhythm lies a slower, more intimate side of life. Staying along the shaded riverbanks, enjoying freshly harvested tropical fruits, tasting local fish dishes, or drifting through narrow canals by sampan reveals a completely different atmosphere. Visitors soon discover that the city’s modern image is only one layer; the true charm of Can Tho is found in its peaceful mornings, relaxed afternoons in garden hammocks, and tranquil evenings filled with the sounds of nature. Beneath the bright skies of the Mekong Delta, Can Tho creates a memorable and authentic travel experience. 

**2\. Top Things to Do**

**Explore the islets**

The small islets scattered along the river offer a glimpse into traditional Mekong Delta life. Travelling by bicycle or boat, visitors can pass through quiet villages, visit fruit orchards, and observe local crafts such as rice paper making. Many community-based tours allow travellers to share a home-cooked meal with local families, providing not only cultural insight but also a deeper connection with the region.

**Visit the floating markets at dawn**

A trip to Can Tho would be incomplete without witnessing the vibrant floating markets. The famous Cai Rang Floating Market comes alive early in the morning, filled with boats selling fresh produce and regional specialties. For a more authentic and less crowded experience, travellers can explore smaller markets nearby, where interactions with local vendors feel more personal and genuine.

**Taste Hu Tieu on a boat ** 

One of the most unique culinary experiences is having breakfast directly on a boat. A bowl of Hu Tieu, a well-known southern Vietnamese noodle dish, is prepared fresh and served hot on the river. The combination of chewy noodles, crisp bean sprouts, and tender slices of pork in a light, flavorful broth creates a simple yet unforgettable meal, enhanced by the lively atmosphere of the market.

**Discover Binh Thuy Ancient House**

Step back into history at Binh Thuy Ancient House, a beautifully preserved residence dating back to the 19th century. This house reflects a unique blend of Eastern and Western architectural styles, showcasing how wealthy families once lived in the region. Antique furniture, decorative details, and its historical significance make it a fascinating cultural stop.

**Relax by the riverside**

Can Tho is also an ideal destination for relaxation. Riverside accommodations provide a peaceful escape from the busy city centre. Visitors can choose from luxury resorts like Azerai Can Tho, well-known for its serene setting, or Victoria Can Tho Resort, which offers a blend of comfort and local charm. For a more eco-friendly and immersive experience, homestays such as Mekong Rustic allow guests to live close to nature and local communities.

**3\. Weather in Can Tho**

Can Tho experiences a typical tropical climate with two main seasons. The dry season, from around December to April, is generally hot but more comfortable for outdoor activities. The rainy season, lasting from May to November, brings frequent showers, especially between July and October. Despite the rain, this period also highlights the lush greenery of the Mekong Delta and offers a different kind of beauty.

**4\. Transport** 

Can Tho is easily accessible by air and road, with Can Tho International Airport and regular buses from Ho Chi Minh City. However, what makes transport in Can Tho truly unique is its extensive waterway system along the Hau River. Boats and sampans are not only a means of transport but also a key part of daily life. Travelling by boat allows visitors to reach floating markets, explore canals, and access remote islets—making it one of the most authentic ways to experience the Mekong Delta.

**5\. Getting Around Can Tho** 

Reaching Can Tho is relatively convenient. Can Tho International Airport connects the city with major destinations across Vietnam. Travellers can also take a bus journey of approximately four hours from Ho Chi Minh City. For a more scenic option, river cruises along the Mekong River provide a unique way to arrive.

Within the city, taxis and ride-hailing services are widely available. Cycling is a great choice for exploring rural areas and nearby islets at a slower pace. Additionally, local ferries operate regularly, making it easy to travel between the mainland and surrounding islands.
