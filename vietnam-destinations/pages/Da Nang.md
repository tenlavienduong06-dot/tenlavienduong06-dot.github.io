### **1\. Introduction**

Da Nang is widely celebrated as the "most livable city in Vietnam," strategically located in the South Central region where majestic mountains, fertile plains, and the vast blue sea converge. In 2026, the city enters a transformative era as a Global Heritage and Technology Hub following its historic administrative merger with Quang Nam province. The vibe of Da Nang is a seamless blend of grand modern infrastructure and rugged natural beauty, ranging from the shimmering Han River to the pristine primary forests of the Son Tra Peninsula. Visitors are drawn here to witness a city evolving into the "Singapore of Vietnam," where cutting-edge smart tourism meets the timeless charm of surrounding UNESCO World Heritage sites. Whether you are seeking the majestic heights of the new Landmark 69 tower or the tranquil healing atmosphere of ecological zones, Da Nang offers a vibrant, world-class experience.

### **2\. Top Things to Do** 

### **Experience & Adventure**

Son Tra Dawn Trekking: Embark on a journey at 5:30 AM to catch the sunrise and witness the rare Red-shanked Douc Langur in its natural habitat.

Hoa Bac Eco-Exploration: Experience "healing" tourism by kayaking on the crystal-clear Cu De River or trekking through the Giàn Bí – Nam Yên forest trails.

Heli-Tourism: For a truly grand perspective, take a luxury helicopter tour to admire the city’s five iconic bridges and coastline from above.

Water Sports at My Khe: Engage in adrenaline-pumping activities like surfing, SUP paddling, and paragliding at one of the world's most beautiful beaches.

**Culture & Heritage**

Michelin Culinary Revolution: Indulge in a "Food Tour" of the city's 36 Michelin-recognized establishments, ranging from the 1-star fine dining at La Maison 1888 to authentic street food like Mì Quảng Sứa Hồng Vân.

Museum of Cham Sculpture: Explore the world's largest collection of Cham artifacts, now enhanced with smart multilingual audio guides and interactive displays.

Nam O Ancient Village: Visit this 700-year-old village to experience the life of local fishermen and see the stunning green moss-covered reefs.

Co Tu Cultural Immersion: Discover the traditional Gươl houses and brocade weaving of the Co Tu ethnic people in the Tà Lang and Giàn Bí communities.

**Landmarks**

Landmark 69 & Da Nang Downtown: Marvel at the city’s newest icon, a 408-meter-tall skyscraper inspired by the philosophy of the Five Elements and the traditional Ao Dai.

Dragon Bridge (Cầu Rồng): Witness the spectacular fire and water-breathing show every Friday, Saturday, and Sunday night at 9:00 PM.

Golden Bridge (Cầu Vàng): Walk along the world-famous bridge held by giant mossy hands at Sun World Ba Na Hills.

Lady Buddha at Linh Ung Pagoda: Visit the 67-meter-tall statue, the tallest in Vietnam, which stands as a spiritual beacon overlooking the bay.

### **3\. Best Time to Visit**

Da Nang features two distinct seasons: the dry season and the rainy season:

February to April (The Pleasant Season): This is an ideal time for sightseeing with cool, comfortable temperatures ranging from 22°C to 28°C and light sunshine.

May to August (The Peak Season): The weather is hot and sunny (up to 35°C), which is perfect for beach activities and enjoying the blue sea. This period also hosts the Da Nang International Fireworks Festival (DIFF 2026), running from late May to mid-July.

September to December (The Rainy Season): Rainfall increases significantly during this time.

Weather Warning: Travelers should absolutely avoid October and November, as these are the peak months for storms and floods, which can lead to service closures and safety risks.

### **4\. Transport & Getting Around**

**Getting There**

By Flight: The fastest method, taking approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes from Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City. Da Nang International Airport is conveniently located only 5km from the city center.

By Train: A scenic journey lasting 15 to 20 hours. It is a safe and budget-friendly option that allows you to admire Vietnam's beautiful coastline and mountain passes.

By Bus: Sleeper buses from major cities take about 16 to 20 hours with fares starting from 300,000 VND.

**Getting Around**

Motorbike Rental: The most flexible way to explore, costing between 100,000 and 200,000 VND per day. It is highly recommended for exploring the Son Tra Peninsula or the Hai Van Pass.

Ride-Hailing & Taxis: Services like Grab, Gojek, and Xanh SM are widely available, providing convenient and transparent pricing for city travel.

Private Car Rental: Ideal for families or groups wanting a comfortable tour to distant sites like Ba Na Hills or Hoi An, with prices around 1,000,000 VND per day.

Cyclo (Xích lô): A unique, friendly tourist product for a slow-paced tour of the inner city streets.
