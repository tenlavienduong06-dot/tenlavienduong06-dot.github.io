**HẠ LONG**

**Overview**  
For many people, Ha Long Bay is almost a symbol of Vietnam. Cruise boats glide across the emerald waters, weaving through thousands of limestone islands and islets with unique shapes. Along the way, they stop at stunning caves where visitors can explore and admire impressive rock formations that have developed over hundreds of years. The bay’s mysterious and breathtaking beauty has made it a must-visit destination, yet it still offers quiet, hidden corners for those seeking a more peaceful experience.

**Top things to do in Ha Long Bay**

**Cruise the bay**  
There is nothing quite like watching the sunset over the calm waters of Ha Long Bay and waking up to a peaceful morning surrounded by towering karsts. Relaxing on the deck while enjoying the wide, scenic views is often the highlight of the trip.

**Explore a floating village**  
Ha Long Bay is not only a UNESCO World Heritage Site but also home to fishing communities that have lived on the water for generations. Taking a boat tour around a floating village gives you a closer look at this unique and fascinating way of life.

**Kayaking**  
Even if you are not experienced, kayaking is an activity you should not miss. It allows you to explore the bay at your own pace, especially during sunset when the lagoons are quiet and the fishing boats are nearby.

**Visit caves**  
Hidden beneath the limestone mountains and lush greenery are many caves formed by natural erosion over time. Some are narrow and adventurous, while others are large and breathtaking, all offering a glimpse into the bay’s geological wonders.

**Rock climbing**  
For adventure lovers, Ha Long Bay offers many climbing routes. Deep water soloing, a form of climbing above the sea without ropes, is becoming increasingly popular, especially in Ha Long Bay and nearby Lan Ha Bay.

**Ha Long Weather**  
From September to November, the weather is cool with clear skies, making it an ideal time to visit. From December to March, mist often covers the bay, creating a more mysterious atmosphere. April and May bring sunshine and a gentle breeze, while the rainy season from June to August can make travel plans less predictable.

**Transportation**  
Most visitors choose package tours that include transportation from Hanoi. Independent travelers can take a bus to Ha Long City or Hai Phong, then use a taxi to reach the port. Another option is taking a seaplane from Noi Bai International Airport, which takes about 45 minutes. Traveling within the bay is best done by boat, while a short seaplane ride offers a stunning bird’s-eye view. In Ha Long City, taxis are a convenient way to get around.
