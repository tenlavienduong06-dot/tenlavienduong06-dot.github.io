About

A World Above the Clouds

Nestled in the Hoàng Liên Sơn mountain range near the Chinese border, Sa Pa is a captivating highland town that enchants visitors with its dramatic scenery, vibrant hill tribe cultures, and misty mountain landscapes. Rising to an altitude of about 1,600m above sea level, Sa Pa serves as the gateway to Fansipan — Vietnam's highest peak and "Roof of Indochina."


The surrounding valleys are blanketed with some of Southeast Asia's most spectacular terraced rice fields, painstakingly carved into the mountainsides by the Hmong, Dao, Tay, Giay, and Xa Pho ethnic minority peoples over centuries. During harvest season, the terraces turn a breathtaking shade of gold.

Top Attractions

Must-See Highlights

Fansipan Peak

The "Roof of Indochina" at 3,143m. Accessible by cable car or a challenging 2-day trek through pristine cloud forests.

Muong Hoa Valley

Stunning terraced rice fields between Sa Pa town and the villages of Lao Chai and Ta Van. Best during September–November harvest.

Ethnic Village Trek

Walk through Hmong and Dao villages like Cát Cát, Lao Chai, and Ta Van. Experience traditional textiles, local markets, and ceremonies.

Culinary Experience

Local Food & Dining

🐑
Thắng Cố

A traditional stew from the Hmong people, made with horse meat and vegetables. A true highland delicacy.

🐟
Grilled Salmon

Fresh Sa Pa salmon grilled over charcoal with local herbs. The cold mountain streams produce exceptionally flavored fish.

🍵
Rượu Ngô (Corn Wine)

Locally brewed corn liquor with a distinctive smoky, sweet flavor. A traditional drink shared during ceremonies and festivals.
